Bellingham: College Town on the Bay

Bellingham is home to Western Washington University, known for it's education and arts programs. It is located about 22 miles south of the Canadian border, and roughly 35 miles from Mt. Baker, making it a hub for adventurers and nature-lovers.

Quick facts about Bellingham:

  • Population: 91,482 as of the 2020 cenus
  • Incorporated in 1903
  • Bellingham is located in the North Cascades region of Washington state, between Bellingham bay and Lake Whatcom.
  • The city is classified as urban due to the due to it being the largest city in Whatcom County and having a mostly dense downtown environment.
  • The average household income as of 2021 is $36,147.
